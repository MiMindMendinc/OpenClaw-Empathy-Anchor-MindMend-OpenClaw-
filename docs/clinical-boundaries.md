# Clinical Boundaries

HumaniCare AI and OpenClaw Empathy Anchor must stay honest about what they are.

They are privacy-first support infrastructure. They are not medical devices, not clinicians, not diagnosis engines, and not emergency services.

## Safer language

Use these terms:

- clinician-informed
- safety-focused
- privacy-first support infrastructure
- wellness support
- resource routing
- youth-aware language
- human-in-the-loop escalation

Avoid these terms unless formally proven:

- clinically validated
- HIPAA compliant
- FDA approved
- diagnostic
- treatment
- therapy replacement
- guaranteed safety

## Standard disclaimer

This project provides educational, reflective, and supportive software patterns. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, therapy, emergency care, or a replacement for licensed professionals.

If someone may be in immediate danger, contact emergency services or a trusted crisis resource immediately.

## Validation path

A credible validation path should include:

1. clinician or advisor review
2. safety policy documentation
3. red-team testing
4. usability testing
5. pilot feedback
6. evidence log
7. independent review when possible

## Why this matters

Strong claims can attract attention, but careful claims build trust.

HumaniCare should be bold about privacy, local-first design, and human-first safety, while staying careful around clinical and legal claims.
